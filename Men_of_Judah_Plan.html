What Is Men of Judah?

Men of Judah is a structured men’s ministry that equips young men at Epicenter of Worship with biblical wisdom and real entrepreneurial skills — producing prayer warriors, business owners, and Kingdom leaders.

We run in 6-month cohorts every Monday evening. Every cohort ends with each member having an active LLC and a 90-day business plan. We build brotherhood through accountability partnerships, expert guest speakers, and a shared commitment to generational wealth.

Who We Serve

Young men (teens through young adults) at Epicenter of Worship. Expansion to full male congregation over time.

When We Meet

Every Monday evening — 90-minute structured sessions. Consistent, recurring, non-negotiable.

The Name

Judah = praise. The tribe of warriors, kings, and Jesus’ lineage. We carry that identity into the marketplace.

The Standard

Every graduate: a filed LLC, business bank account, 90-day plan, and a brotherhood of men who know his name.

The Problem We’re Solving

Men are leaving the churchMen disengage from faith communities at higher rates than women. We give them a reason to show up — and stay.
Financial illiteracy is a crisisMost men in our congregation have no LLC, no investment account, no will, no trust — and no one teaching them why they need these.
The wealth gap is spiritual tooGenerational poverty is not just an economic issue. It is a discipleship gap. We close it with both Scripture and strategy.
Young men need modelsThe most powerful thing we can offer a young man is a room full of men building something real, in God’s name.

Mission & Core Values

MISSION: Men of Judah exists to activate young men in the church, equip them with biblical wisdom and entrepreneurial skills, and send them into the marketplace as leaders, wealth builders, and Kingdom ambassadors — beginning at Epicenter of Worship and expanding across Lansing and beyond.

Curriculum: Purpose to Profit

Four modules over one 5-month cohort. Each module has a scriptural anchor, a real-world skill component, and a weekly action item.

Module 1 — AugustIdentity & Purpose
  • DISC Assessment & debrief
  • Spiritual Gifts Inventory
  • Purpose Assessment Worksheet
  • Biblical economics & stewardship
  • Guest: Pastor-entrepreneur
OUTCOME: Personal “Why I’m Building” statement completed
Module 2 — SeptemberBusiness Foundations
  • What is an LLC and why you need one
  • Michigan LLC filing walkthrough (live in-session)
  • Business banking: opening your account
  • Business structures: LLC vs. S-Corp
  • Guest: CPA or tax professional
OUTCOME: LLC filed. Business bank account opened.
Module 3 — OctoberWealth Mechanics
  • IUL strategy — using life insurance as your bank
  • Trusts: what they are and why you need one
  • Roth IRA and brokerage basics
  • Real estate 101: house hacking, wholesaling
  • Guest: Financial advisor & real estate investor
OUTCOME: Investment account opened or plan written.
Module 4 — Nov–DecAI, Tech & Market Positioning
  • AI tools every entrepreneur needs right now
  • Building income streams with AI
  • Social media as a business development tool
  • Personal brand that attracts clients
  • Guest: Tech entrepreneur or content creator
OUTCOME: GRADUATION — Present LLC + 90-day business plan
GRADUATION STANDARD: Every member who completes a cohort will have: a filed LLC, a business bank account, a written 90-day plan, and a brother in this room who knows their business by name.

Long-Term Vision

Men of Judah is designed to be more than a church group. It is a deployable program with a citywide mandate.

1
Epicenter of Worship (2026) — Prove the model. Trial Sprint + Cohort 1. Document wins and refine the curriculum.
2
Lansing Church Network (2027) — Launch in 2–3 partner churches using Cohort 1 alumni as lead facilitators.
3
City-Wide Program (2027–2028) — Men of Judah becomes a Lansing institution. Community center partnerships.
4
Church Think Tank — Alumni advisory body of business owners driving community economic strategy and mentorship pipeline.
5
Community Financial Institution (Long-Term) — A church-seeded credit union or CDFI to provide capital within the community. 5–7 year horizon.
